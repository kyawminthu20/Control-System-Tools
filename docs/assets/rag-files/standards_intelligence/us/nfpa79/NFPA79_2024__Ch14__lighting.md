## 0. Scope

This chapter covers the electrical supply, wiring, and hardware requirements for **machine task lighting** and **internal control panel lighting**. It ensures that the lighting systems do not introduce new electrical hazards (such as shock or fire) and that they are robust enough to withstand the mechanical stresses (vibration, heat, and fluids) of an industrial environment.

## 1. Panel and Machine Lighting Rules

To comply with the 2024 edition, lighting systems must adhere to specific electrical and physical standards:

* **Supply Source:** * Lighting circuits should ideally be connected to the load side of the main disconnect.
* If the lighting must remain on when the main disconnect is off (for maintenance safety), it must be fed from a separate, clearly labeled disconnect or a "line-side" tap that is appropriately protected and shielded.


* **Voltage Limits:** Task lighting is generally limited to **120V AC** or less. The use of **24V DC LED** lighting has become the preferred standard because it falls under the "touch-safe" PELV/SELV thresholds discussed in Chapter 7.
* **Protection and Durability:**
* **Lamps:** Must be protected against physical damage. For machine-mounted lights, this usually requires oil-tight housings and tempered glass or impact-resistant lenses.
* **Stroboscopic Effect:** Lighting must be designed or positioned so that it does not create a stroboscopic effect that makes rotating parts (like a drill or saw) appear stationary or slow-moving.


* **Control:** * Panel lighting must be controlled by a dedicated switch (either manual or a door-activated limit switch).
* In the 2024 edition, there is an emphasis on ensuring that the placement of these switches does not require a technician to reach over "live" uninsulated parts.
