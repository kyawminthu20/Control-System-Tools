# IEC 60204-1 (2016+AMD1:2021) — Clause 1 — Scope

## 0. Purpose

The purpose of IEC 60204-1 is to provide requirements and recommendations relating to the electrical equipment of machines so as to promote:

* **Safety of persons and property:** Preventing electric shock and fire.
* **Consistency of control response:** Ensuring the machine acts predictably (functional safety).
* **Ease of maintenance:** Standardizing wiring and documentation for global serviceability.

## 1. In-Scope Machinery

The standard applies to electrical equipment and systems of machines that operate in the **non-portable** category while working. It specifically covers:

* **Voltage Limits:** Systems operating with nominal supply voltages not exceeding **1000V AC** or **1500V DC**.
* **Frequencies:** Nominal frequencies not exceeding **200 Hz**.
* **Examples:** Metalworking machinery (lathes, mills), plastics machinery, wood-processing machines, assembly robots, and automated material handling systems (conveyors).

## 2. Out-of-Scope Installations

Certain types of electrical equipment are excluded because they are covered by more specialized standards:

* **High-Voltage Power:** Systems exceeding the 1000VAC/1500VDC limits.
* **Underground Mining:** Equipment intended for use in explosive underground environments.
* **Public Infrastructures:** Machinery intended for use in open-air public areas (e.g., amusement park rides).
* **Extreme Environments:** Machinery in highly corrosive or explosive (ATEX) atmospheres often requires IEC 60204-1 **plus** additional standards like IEC 60079.

## 3. Relationship to NFPA 79

While both standards aim for machine safety, they have distinct regional characteristics:

* **Harmonization:** There is a high degree of technical alignment between the two, but they are not identical.
* **Component Ratings:** NFPA 79 heavily references **UL** listing and **NEC** installation rules. IEC 60204-1 relies on **CE marking** (in Europe) and the **IEC 60364** series for installations.
* **Color Codes:** IEC 60204-1 uses different wiring color conventions (e.g., Light Blue for Neutral, whereas NFPA 79 uses White).
