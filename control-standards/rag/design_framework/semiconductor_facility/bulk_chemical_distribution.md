# Bulk Chemical Distribution and Wet Process Systems

## Purpose and boundary

This note covers facility-side liquid chemical infrastructure:

- bulk storage and tank farms
- day tanks
- transfer skids
- blend or dilution skids
- wet benches and local supply panels
- leak detection and secondary containment interfaces

## Main engineering objectives

- protect people and facility from chemical release
- maintain chemical purity and compatibility
- transfer material without cross-contamination
- prevent pump damage, overfill, and incompatible routing
- support safe drain segregation and waste handling

## Main engineering concerns

### Material compatibility

- define wetted materials for every chemical family
- include concentration and temperature, not just chemical name
- consider tubing, valve seats, diaphragms, sensor bodies, gaskets, and sample lines

### Containment and leak response

- provide secondary containment where appropriate
- treat leak-detection zoning as part of process design
- decide what shuts down locally and what escalates to facility response

### Transfer sequencing

- verify source available
- verify destination ready
- verify correct line-up
- verify no active leak or overfill condition
- verify required exhaust or ventilation status where applicable

## Typical instrumentation

- tank level and independent overfill
- transfer flow and line pressure
- pump status and fault
- temperature where compatibility or blend quality depends on it
- leak detection
- concentration or chemistry analyzers where required by the process

## Control philosophy highlights

- interlock pump start against closed-path or low-level conditions
- block chemical transfer on active leak or destination not ready
- isolate hazardous flows on leak detection or emergency commands
- treat drains and flushes as controlled sequence steps, not informal operator actions

## Common document outputs

- chemical compatibility matrix
- bulk-to-day-tank transfer narrative
- leak-detection zoning map
- drain segregation matrix
- overfill and spill response table

## Standards anchors

- SEMI F39 for chemical blending systems
- SEMI F57 for polymer materials and components used in UPW and liquid chemical distribution
- SEMI C90 for fluorinated materials in liquid chemical distribution systems
- SEMI F31 and F41 where bulk chemical distribution qualification becomes relevant
- SEMI S2 for equipment safety framing
- NFPA and local fire or building code where storage quantities and hazard classes matter
