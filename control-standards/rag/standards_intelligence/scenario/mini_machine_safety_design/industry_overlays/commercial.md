# Commercial Overlay

## What Changes vs Baseline

- First verify that the product is still being treated as industrial machinery. If it is instead being treated as a building-service package or utility assembly, the primary code pathway may shift away from the baseline NFPA 79 machinery model. [LOCAL: us/nfpa79/NFPA79_2024__Ch01__administration.md#1-applicability-rules] [LOCAL: us/nec/NEC_2023__Art409__industrial_control_panels.md#1-panel-definition-and-scope]
- Commercial installations often bring stronger emphasis on labeling, working space, disconnect identification, and maintainability for general facility personnel. [LOCAL: us/nec/NEC_2023__Art110__requirements_for_electrical_installations.md#1-installation--listing-requirements-field-rules] [LOCAL: us/nec/NEC_2023__Art110__requirements_for_electrical_installations.md#4-control-panel-design-implications]
- Customer-required listing, commissioning, and maintenance documents should be formalized early. [LOCAL: us/nec/NEC_2023__Art409__industrial_control_panels.md#4-relationship-to-ul-508a]

## Standards To Prioritize

- NEC Articles 110, 409, 250, 240, 310, and 300 [LOCAL: us/nec/NEC_2023__Art110__requirements_for_electrical_installations.md#0-scope-and-relevance-to-control-panels]
- NFPA 79 only where the system still qualifies as industrial machinery [LOCAL: routing/standards_applicability.md#general-industrial-machinery]
- UL listing pathway if required by the customer or AHJ [LOCAL: us/ul_508a/UL508A_OVERVIEW.md#when-ul-508a-applies]

## Typical Inspection / Acceptance Artifacts

- panel listing or field-evaluation plan
- nameplate and label schedule
- installation clearance verification
- O&M manual
- site commissioning checklist
