## 0. Purpose

The primary purpose of Chapter 5 is to provide the requirements for the **Machine Supply Circuit Disconnecting Means**. It ensures that every machine has a dedicated point where all electrical energy can be isolated, preventing accidental startup and protecting personnel during service or repair.

## 1. Main Disconnect Requirements

Each machine must be provided with a single main disconnect (unless specific exceptions apply for very large, multi-feed systems).

* **Permitted Types:** The disconnect must be one of the following:
* A listed motor circuit switch.
* A listed molded case circuit breaker.
* A listed molded case switch.


* **Operating Handle:** The handle must be mechanically linked to the disconnect and must be **external** to the enclosure, allowing an operator to kill power without opening the door.
* **Visual Indication:** The handle must clearly indicate whether it is in the **"ON" (I)** or **"OFF" (O)** position.

## 2. Location and Accessibility

The placement of the disconnect is critical for safety:

* **Line of Sight:** Ideally, the disconnect should be located within sight of the machine it controls.
* **Height:** The center of the grip of the operating handle, when in its highest position, must not be more than **2.0 meters (6 ft 7 in.)** above the floor.
* **Ease of Access:** It must be "readily accessible," meaning it cannot be blocked by pallets, conveyors, or other permanent structures.

## 3. Lockout / Tagout (LOTO) Implications

NFPA 79 heavily reinforces OSHA's LOTO requirements:

* **Padlockable:** The disconnect must be capable of being locked in the **OFF** position only. It must be designed so that a padlock can be applied regardless of whether a technician is present (the "lockable" mechanism must be a permanent part of the switch).
* **Multiple Locks:** The mechanism must typically accommodate more than one padlock for cases where multiple technicians are working on the same machine.

## 4. Control Panel Design Rules

The disconnect interacts directly with the physical design of the control cabinet:

* **Interlocking:** The enclosure door must be **mechanically or electrically interlocked** with the disconnect. This ensures that the door cannot be opened while the power is ON, and the power cannot be turned ON while the door is open (unless a specialized "defeater" mechanism is used by qualified personnel).
* **Line-Side Protection:** The "line side" terminals (where power enters the cabinet) must be shielded or guarded to prevent accidental contact even when the main disconnect is in the OFF position. These are often marked with "WARNING: LIVE TERMINALS" labels.
