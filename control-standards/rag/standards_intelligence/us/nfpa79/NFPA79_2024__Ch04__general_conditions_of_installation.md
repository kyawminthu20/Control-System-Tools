## 0. Environmental Considerations

The electrical equipment must be capable of operating correctly under the specific conditions of its intended installation site.

* **Ambient Temperature:** Standard equipment must operate in air temperatures between ** and ** ( to ). If the environment is hotter (e.g., near a furnace) or colder (e.g., a cold storage facility), specialized cooling or heating systems must be integrated.
* **Altitude:** Equipment is generally rated for use up to **1000 meters** (3300 feet). Above this, the thinning air reduces the effectiveness of convection cooling and changes the dielectric properties of the air (arcing risk).
* **Contaminants:** The design must account for "Expected Contaminants" such as oil mist, corrosive chemicals, or conductive dust. This directly dictates the required NEMA or IP rating of the enclosures.

## 1. Accessibility and Maintainability

A machine that cannot be safely serviced is a non-compliant machine. Chapter 4 mandates:

* **Clearance:** There must be sufficient "working space" around control panels to allow for examination, adjustment, and maintenance. This generally aligns with **NEC Article 110.26** (typically 3 feet of clear depth).
* **Illumination:** If the machine's internal lighting is insufficient for safe maintenance, the manufacturer must provide integral lighting or specify requirements for external task lighting.
* **Mounting Height:** Operating handles for disconnects and primary control buttons should generally be located between **0.6 meters and 1.9 meters** (2 feet to 6.25 feet) above the floor level for ergonomic and emergency access.

## 2. Enclosure Implications

The enclosure is the primary defense against the environment and accidental contact with live parts.

* **Protection Against Ingression:** Enclosures must be selected based on the environment (e.g., NEMA 12 for dust/oil-tight, NEMA 4X for wash-down/corrosion resistance).
* **Access Control:** All enclosures containing "live parts" operating at 50V or more must require a **tool or key** for opening, or be interlocked with the main disconnect so that power is removed before the door can be opened.
* **Internal Condensation:** In environments with high humidity or temperature swings, the standard requires means to prevent condensation (such as breathers or space heaters) to avoid short circuits.
