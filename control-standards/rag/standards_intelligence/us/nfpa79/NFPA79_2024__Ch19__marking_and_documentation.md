## 0. Scope

This chapter specifies the requirements for **nameplates, warning signs, and technical documentation** that must accompany the industrial machine. It bridges the gap between the manufacturer's design and the end-user's safety program (LOTO). The 2024 edition places additional emphasis on the visibility and durability of these markings under industrial conditions.

## 1. Labeling Requirements

The standard requires specific physical markings on the machine and its components:

* **Machine Nameplate:** Every machine must have a permanent nameplate located near the main disconnect. It must include:
* Manufacturer’s name.
* Serial number or model designation.
* Voltage, frequency, and number of phases.
* Full-load current (FLA).
* **Short-Circuit Current Rating (SCCR)** of the machine control panel.


* **Warning Signs:** Safety signs must be used to warn of hazards such as "High Voltage," "Multiple Power Sources," or "Arc Flash." These must follow **ANSI Z535** standards for color and signal words (DANGER, WARNING, CAUTION).
* **Component Identification:** Every device inside the panel (relays, PLCs, breakers) must be labeled with a reference designation (e.g., CR101, MTR1) that matches the electrical schematics.

## 2. Documentation Expectations

"Documentation" is not just a manual; it is a safety requirement. NFPA 79 requires that a complete set of technical documentation be provided in a format agreed upon by the user and supplier.

* **Electrical Schematics:** Must be provided and must use standardized symbols (NFPA/IEEE or IEC). They must include a clear representation of all circuits, including safety interlocks.
* **Interlock Diagrams:** For complex machines, the documentation must describe the sequence of operation and the function of safety interlocks.
* **Maintenance Instructions:** Must include specific procedures for the safe maintenance of the electrical equipment and a list of recommended spare parts.
* **Calculation Records:** Documentation of the SCCR calculations and, in recent editions, the results of safety function validation (if using functional safety software) should be accessible.
