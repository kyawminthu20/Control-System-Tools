# UL 508A — Marking and Documentation

## 0. Scope
Marking and documentation requirements ensure a panel can be identified, applied, inspected, and maintained without opening the enclosure and guessing at its basis of design.

## 1. Required panel markings
The finished panel should carry a permanent external marking or nameplate with the core information needed to identify and apply it correctly.

Typical required or expected data includes:

- manufacturer identity
- a serial, catalog, or other unique panel identifier
- supply ratings such as voltage, phase, and frequency
- full-load or maximum current information as applicable
- SCCR
- enclosure type designation
- grounding terminal identification where required

In many projects, additional field-useful items such as a drawing number or largest motor value are also included because they improve inspection and service traceability.

## 2. Documentation retention
Documentation should support how the panel was actually built. Useful retained records typically include:

- schematic and layout drawings
- bill of materials
- component instructions and listing conditions where needed
- SCCR basis and calculation method
- nameplate basis data

This documentation is what allows a shop, inspector, or service team to explain why the marked values on the panel are valid.

## 3. Audit readiness
Two practical distinctions are important during audit or inspection:

- the enclosure's own label is not the same as the industrial control panel listing mark
- the panel listing mark does not replace the need for a complete external nameplate

Good audit readiness also includes conductor identification and documentation traceability so a technician can connect the physical panel back to the drawings without reconstructing the entire build from scratch.
