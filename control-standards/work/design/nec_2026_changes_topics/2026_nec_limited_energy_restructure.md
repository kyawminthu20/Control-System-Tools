# 2026 NEC Limited Energy Restructure

## What this file is

This is a cleaned work note derived from the limited-energy restructuring section of `2026 NEC Changes.md` (raw source note removed from the repository).

Approximate source range:

- `24:36` to `34:28`

## Topic focus

This segment explains how the 2026 NEC groups limited-energy systems into a more unified chapter structure.

## Main concepts captured

### 1. New definition of limited energy system

- The transcript says a limited-energy system is either restricted by output power or is managed so hazards of fire and electric shock are mitigated by the system design.
- Class `2`, Class `3`, Class `4`, communications, and power-limited fire alarm concepts are all pulled into this broader umbrella.

### 2. Article 720 becomes the general-rules article

- The speaker describes `720` as a limited-energy counterpart to the normal Article `300` style of general wiring-method rules.
- The goal is to stop forcing users to bounce back and forth between Article `300` and a long list of exceptions.

### 3. Supporting limited-energy articles are grouped together

The transcript identifies this limited-energy structure:

- `720` general requirements
- `721` power sources
- `722` cable requirements
- `723` raceways, cable routing assemblies, and cable trays
- `742` overvoltage protection
- `750` grounding and bonding

### 4. Chapter 8 content is heavily absorbed or reduced

- The transcript says Articles `770` and `805` are removed as stand-alone homes for their old subject matter.
- Article `800` is reduced mainly to outdoor and entering-building communication systems.
- Article `840` is also largely absorbed or reduced in scope.

### 5. The larger goal is technology-agnostic grouping

- The speaker explicitly wants the code to stop splitting rules based on legacy labels when the actual installation risks and cable rules are basically the same.
- The organizing question becomes: is it limited energy, and if so, which limited-energy article handles the specific aspect?

## Working takeaway

This segment is mainly about making limited-energy rules easier to follow:

- fewer duplicated articles
- cleaner grouping by function
- less back-and-forth between old communication and signaling article families
