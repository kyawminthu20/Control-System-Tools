# DC Motor Commutator, Brushes, and Power Path

## Topic focus

This segment explains how electrical power is transferred into the rotating armature of a DC motor.

## Main concepts captured

### 1. The commutator solves the rotating-connection problem

- Because the rotor turns, fixed conductors cannot directly feed the armature windings.
- The commutator and brush system provides the sliding electrical interface needed to energize the rotor continuously.

### 2. The commutator is a segmented copper switching surface

- The source describes thin copper commutator segments clamped between end rings and insulated with mica.
- Segment count depends on the armature-slot count and winding multiplicity.

### 3. Risers connect commutator segments to the winding ends

- Conductive risers bridge the smaller commutator diameter to the armature coil ends.
- Insulating spacers keep adjacent risers electrically separated.

### 4. Brushes provide the stationary-to-rotating current interface

- Graphite-based brushes are described as the common industrial choice.
- Brush holders, springs, and pressure fingers maintain contact pressure against the commutator surface.
- Flexible copper conductors embedded in the brushes help carry current and reduce localized wear.

### 5. Brush arms and bus bars distribute the supply by alternating polarity

- The transcript explains that bus bars feed the brush arms.
- The brush-arm polarity alternates in a pattern matching the alternating pole polarity of the stator system.
- Power then flows from bus bars to brush arms, to brushes, to commutator segments, through risers, and finally into the armature coils.

### 6. The brush assembly is mechanically isolated and rigidly supported

- The whole brush rig is mounted on a support disc.
- Insulated mounting details are used so the assembly remains mechanically secure without unintended electrical contact to the frame.

## Working takeaway

This segment is the connection-path note for the DC motor:

- bus bars feed brushes
- brushes feed the commutator
- the commutator feeds the rotating armature coils in the required sequence
