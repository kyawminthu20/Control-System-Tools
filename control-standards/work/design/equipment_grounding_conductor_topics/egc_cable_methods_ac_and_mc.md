## Topic focus

This segment compares armor cable and MC cable grounding methods, especially where bonding strips are involved.

## Main concepts captured

### 1. AC cable and MC cable are not being treated as the same thing

- The transcript spends time distinguishing armor cable practices from MC cable practices.
- The key difference is how the grounding function is established within the cable assembly.

### 2. Armor cable uses the bonding-strip concept

- The discussion describes AC cable as using the metal armor together with an aluminum bonding strip.
- The teaching point is that the bonding strip helps ensure continuity through the armor.

### 3. MC cable may use a wire-type EGC or a listed bonding-strip system

- The transcript references MC cable with a green wire EGC.
- It also references an MC variation that uses an aluminum bonding conductor/strip arrangement as part of a listed grounding method.

### 4. Handling details matter

- The speaker comments on common field habits around anti-short bushings ("redheads"), bonding strips, and cable termination practices.
- The practical message is that installers should not blindly carry one cable-type habit over to another.

### 5. Listed fittings are part of the system

- The cable itself is not the whole story.
- The transcript emphasizes that the fittings and terminations are part of the listed grounding method.

## Working takeaway

This segment is a good reminder that cable grounding methods are assembly-specific:

- know whether the cable relies on armor, bonding strip, or wire-type EGC
- terminate it with the right listed fitting
- follow the manufacturer instructions instead of copying habits from a different cable type
