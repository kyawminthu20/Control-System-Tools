# EGC Wire and Raceway Methods

## What this file is

This is a cleaned work note derived from the part of the transcript that walks through wire-type and raceway-type equipment grounding conductors in [types of equipment ground conductors.md](../types%20of%20equipment%20ground%20conductors.md).

## Topic focus

This segment covers the common question: which wiring methods can themselves serve as the equipment grounding conductor?

## Main concepts captured

### 1. A wire-type EGC can be many forms

- The transcript notes that a wire-type EGC can be bare or insulated.
- It can also be solid or stranded.

### 2. Metal raceways can serve as the EGC

The transcript treats the following as ordinary recognized EGC paths:

- rigid metal conduit
- IMC
- EMT

### 3. The lesson pushes back on the idea that a separate green wire is always required in metal raceway

- The speaker acknowledges that some industries still install a separate wire-type EGC inside metal raceway for durability or local practice reasons.
- But the transcript emphasizes that listed metal raceways are themselves recognized by code as equipment grounding conductors.

### 4. Flexible metal conduit has conditions

- The transcript says flexible metal conduit is permitted only within specific limitations.
- The main points captured are short length, listed fittings, and low enough overcurrent protection.

### 5. Liquidtight flexible metal conduit also has conditions

- The transcript describes separate size/length/current limitations for liquidtight flexible metal conduit.
- The practical point is that these flexible methods are not treated the same as rigid metal raceways.

### 6. Flexibility can force a wire-type bonding path

- The discussion highlights that when flexibility is actually required, a wire-type equipment grounding conductor or bonding jumper may need to be installed.

## Working takeaway

This segment is mostly about not treating all metal wiring methods as interchangeable:

- rigid metal raceways are broadly accepted as EGCs
- flexible methods are conditional
- listed fittings and continuity matter
- field habit is not the same thing as code recognition
